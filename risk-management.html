Risk Management

Risk management is about staying in the game long enough to let your edge play out. A trader who respects risk can survive long enough to learn and improve.

Core Rules

  • Risk a small fixed percent per trade (for example 0.5–1% of the account).
  • Size positions so that a full stop-out equals your planned risk.
  • Set a daily loss limit to avoid emotional revenge trading.

Risk–Reward & Position Sizing Formula

Good trades aim for at least 2R or 3R (2 or 3 times reward vs. risk). 

Position Size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Stop Distance × Value Per Point)

Example: Account $5,000, risk 1% = $50. If stop loss is 25 points and each point is $1, then Position Size = 50 ÷ 25 = 2 contracts.

Expectancy & Journaling

Expectancy = (Win% × Avg Win) − (Loss% × Avg Loss)

Tracking expectancy turns trading into a measurable process instead of guessing. Journaling helps you see whether you follow your rules or fall into emotional decisions.

  • Why did you take the trade?
  • Was it with or against trend?
  • Did you follow your SL/TP rules?
  • Did emotions influence your exit?
