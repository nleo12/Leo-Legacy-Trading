Key Concepts

These ideas form the foundation of the Leo Legacy Trading curriculum. Each card below includes a short practice idea so the concept can be applied, not just memorized.

1. Market Structure

Identify swing highs/lows, trends, and breaks in structure across multiple timeframes.

Practice: Print or screenshot charts and label the structure daily.

2. Price Action Setups

Use candles and patterns (such as break-and-retest) to time entries around key levels.

Practice: Save charts of setups you like and write notes on why.

3. Risk Management

Decide how much you are willing to lose before placing a trade, not after.

Practice: Use a fixed % per trade and track your drawdowns.

4. Confluence

Combine levels, indicators, and price action to build higher probability setups.

Practice: Only take trades when at least two conditions line up.

5. Momentum & Indicators

Use tools like RSI and MACD to confirm momentum, not to replace price action.

Practice: Journal when indicators helped or lagged on real charts.

6. Psychological Biases

Recognize fear, greed, overtrading, and revenge trading patterns in your behavior.

Practice: Keep a small psychology log after each trading session.

7. Trading Plan & Journal

Create written rules and track your trades to see if your edge is actually working.

Practice: Set weekly review time to evaluate your data.
