Technical Indicators

Technical indicators are math tools built from price or volume. I treat them as supporting evidence, not as the only reason to enter or exit a trade.

Common Indicators

  • Moving Averages: Smooth price and show trend direction.
  • RSI: Momentum oscillator that can show overbought/oversold and divergence.
  • MACD: Combines EMAs to highlight momentum shifts.
  • Bollinger Bands: Use volatility bands to spot expansion and potential breakouts.

Indicator Use in Real Trading

Indicators should act as confirmation rather than prediction. Professional traders first analyze price structure, volume shifts, and trend direction, then use indicators to validate decisions.

  • RSI helps identify exhaustion points and potential reversals.
  • Moving Averages show trend and dynamic support or resistance.
  • MACD reveals momentum changes and crossover signals.
  • Bollinger Bands show volatility expansion and breakouts.

Example entry: In an uptrend, price pulls into the 50 EMA, RSI crosses back above 40, and MACD turns positive — together they confirm a pullback buying opportunity.

MA + RSI confluence can increase the probability of catching pullbacks safely.
