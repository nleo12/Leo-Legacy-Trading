Risk Management

Risk management is the most important part of trading. A trader who focuses only on entries and ignores risk can blow up an account quickly. A trader who respects risk can survive long enough to develop skill and build a real edge.

Core Risk Rules

  • Risk per Trade: Choose a fixed percentage of your account (0.5%–1%) to risk on each trade.
  • Position Size: Adjust your lot size so that a full stop-loss equals your chosen risk amount.
  • Daily Loss Limit: Set a maximum daily loss to prevent emotional revenge trading.

Position Sizing Example

  1. Account size: $10,000
  2. Risk per trade: 1% = $100
  3. Distance from entry to stop: 20 pips or ticks

To obey your risk rule, calculate a position size where a 20 pip move against you equals $100. This connects math to discipline and prevents oversized positions.

The CME Group and many brokers provide free risk management primers that explain sizing in more depth.

Expectancy & Journaling

Expectancy tells you what you can expect to gain or lose on average per trade, based on your win rate, average win, and average loss.

Expectancy formula:

(Win% × Avg Win) − (Loss% × Avg Loss)

For more background, see the discussion of expectancy in trading psychology books by authors such as Van K. Tharp.
